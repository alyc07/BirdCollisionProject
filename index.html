NEIU Bird Collision Project

The Bird Collision Project at Northeastern Illinois University is a project revolved around collecting deceased birds that have hit windows on campus. A group of students collect these birds every day during the fall and spring migration seasons and keep track of how many birds were collected, species, location, weather conditions and so on. I created a geodatabase for the bird collisions and all of the data collected and mapped out different points as to where the birds collided with the windows and what season. The purple points represent the 2015 spring migration and the blue points repsresent the 2015 fall migration. This is a preliminary map, as I will be adding more points for both of these seasons and other seasons for which data was collected.
